<p><p>Bicycle-sharing systems are categorized into three market types. Point-to-Point systems allow users to pick up a bike at one location and drop it off at another, promoting flexible travel. Ride-and-Return systems require users to return the bike to the original location, fostering a structured usage pattern. Distributed markets feature bikes available across multiple locations, often using smart technology for tracking and management, enhancing accessibility and convenience for users in urban environments. Each type serves distinct mobility needs.</p></p>
