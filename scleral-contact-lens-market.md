<p><p>Scleral contact lenses are large-diameter gas-permeable lenses that rest on the sclera, or the white part of the eye, creating a tear-filled vault over the cornea. They are mainly used to correct vision problems associated with irregular corneas, such as keratoconus or after corneal transplant surgery, and provide greater comfort for individuals with dry eyes or ocular surface disease.</p><p>The scleral contact lens market is experiencing significant growth, driven by rising awareness of vision disorders, increasing adoption due to their therapeutic benefits, and advancements in lens technology. <p><strong>The Scleral Contact Lens Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Rigid Scleral Contact Lens</li><li>Soft Scleral Contact Lens</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The scleral contact lens market includes two main types: rigid scleral contact lenses and soft scleral contact lenses. Rigid scleral lenses are made from durable, oxygen-permeable materials, providing a stable fit over the sclera while creating a tear-filled reservoir that aids in vision correction and comfort for irregular corneas. In contrast, soft scleral lenses are crafted from softer, more flexible materials, offering enhanced comfort and adaptability, making them suitable for a wider range of vision correction needs and eye conditions.</p></p>
