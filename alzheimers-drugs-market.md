<p><p>The Alzheimer's drugs market primarily comprises three types: cholinergic drugs, memantine, and combined drugs. Cholinergic drugs, like donepezil, enhance acetylcholine levels to improve cognitive function. Memantine, on the other hand, regulates glutamate activity, helping to protect neurons and manage symptoms in moderate to severe Alzheimer's. Combined drugs incorporate both mechanisms, offering a more comprehensive approach to treatment. Each category targets different aspects of the disease, catering to varied patient needs and stages of Alzheimer’s progression.</p></p>
